About

Artic is a web application for making and revising annotations aligned with speech recordings, and for vizualzing and correcting derived speech signals that can be used both offline, locally on the users computer, and online in a server environment.

The app supports multiple perspectives to be defined for signal and annotation levels to be presented along with the speech signal timeline (regardless of whether the oscillogram is shown or not). The app also supports a 2D view to be set up in a perspective, to display derived signal movements in two dimensions along the time line.

Artic supports working with speech recordings in most formats and containers, and complex audio formats will be converted by the app directly to make a seamless experience for the user. Some audio format containers, notably MP3, m4a, wma, and other formats desined for efficient storage or streaming of music, may differ in exact timing of percieved sound events compared to the oscillogram signal vizualisation. If exact aligment of acoustic events and the associated annotation labels are of essence, lossless formats (for instance wav or flac) should instead be used.

Historic reference

Artic is an almost complete re-write of the Angular based EMU-webApp develoed for the EMU Speech Database Management System. Please go to http://ips-lmu.github.io/EMU.html or read the EMU-SDMS Manual for more information on this great system.
